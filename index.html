About the club

(Chhitmanthan) is a student-initiated club with a core community of passionate psychology students of the Department of Psychology, University of Allahabad who work to offer the students a platform to interact outside the classroom, and develop an interest and inclination towards the field of psychology. It also aims at serving the wider community throughout-reach activities.

The Motto and its meaning

The club operates with the motto- - Connection, Communication, and Collaboration. It defines the core principles of the club which are –

  • Connect all the students, research scholars and the eminent educationists of psychology
  • ommunicate ideas, thoughts, and reflections and derived learnings on academic and nonacademic issues, and
  • Organize collaborative academic and cultural events, and social out-reach activities.

Mission

With the belief that knowledge can and should essentially breakdown the four walls of the classroom, the clubs aims at proving a stimulating environment that promotes learning and knowledge acquisition. The club is dedicated to promoting the knowledge of psychology by addressing academic and non-academic issues by conducting real-time events, guest lectures, and simulated group discussions. It aims at spreading awareness about the importance of issues related to human psyche, and educate the student community about life and behavioral skills through newsletters and awareness posters on social media and in person

Vision

To build an atmosphere that facilitates peer learning, and help students in their academic and non-academic pursuits so that they can put in practice the knowledge obtained in classes and divulge relevant work to the society.

Objectives

  • To establish an enriching atmosphere that promotes learning, and where members of the club could work towards development their personalities and learn to broaden their knowledge spectrum.
  • To promote the values and visions of the celebrated Indian and Western psychologists.
  • To assist its members in figuring out the best course of action for their career and its success.
  • To provide a common platform for the students and alumni of the Department of Psychology, University of Allahabad to interact and exchange ideas.
  • To print, publish and exhibit newsletters and awarenessposters that may be considered desirable for the promotion of the objective of the club, and not for commercial purposes.

Activities:

The club will organize the following activities on a regular basis (starting October 2019):

  • Group Discussions (Last Friday1 of each month)
  • Conduct quiz (Second Friday2 of every even month)
  • Guest lectures and Seminars
  • Cultural activities on days with significance to Psychology.

Structure and Responsibilities of the Club committees:

  • Executive committee : Shall provide leadership in planning, guiding, directing, and monitoring the activities and tasks given to the other committees and members of the club.
  • Awareness/Outreach/Media committee :Shall communicate the agenda of club meeting, details about the events and activities to the students of the other departments of the universities and institutes. It shall also exhibit newsletters and awareness posters prepared by the editorial committee in person and on social media.
  • (Directorate-of-events) Organizing committee :Organize and manage academic and cultural events and activities along with the monitoring of Psychsabha.
  • Editorial Committee : Shall prepare write-ups on the events and activities the club organizes in the department, and exhibit them with the help of the Awareness committee.
  • Documentation committee :volunteers of this committee will maintain the records of all activities of the club.
  • Quiz Committee :Shall prepare both the quiz questions and its medium of presentation on the day. They shall keep the questions undisclosed till the final day.

Guidelines for the Core Committees:

  • The committees will be of 3-6 students (as per the need) from both M.A. First and Second year.
  • The tenure of volunteers of all the six core committees is of three months.
  • Apart from these six core committees, new short-term committees can be formed and terminated as per the requirement.

Guidelines for the members of the club:

  • Membership of the club is voluntary.
  • All the members have the responsibility of contributing both time and efforts for the club and events and activities it organizes.
  • Under-Graduate members shall consult the executive and any other core committee with respect to their role in the club.
  • Unapproved activities conducted in the name of the club by its members will lead to their membership termination and disciplinary actions by the department.

Club Meetings:

Ethical principles of the club:

  • Maintenance of Individuals’ and the club’s dignity is the first priority of the club and will be observed very strictly.
  • There will no hierarchy amongst the core committees, and between the committees and club members.
  • Volunteers and members are bound to discharge their duties and assigned responsibilities.
